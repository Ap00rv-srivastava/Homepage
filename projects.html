Active structures: Design and Control (May 2019 — July 2019)
Guide: Prof. Ian F. C. Smith, EPFL Switzerland
Dr. Gennaro Senatore, EPFL Switzerland

The project was directed towards developing design and control methodology for an adaptive bridge prototype. Adaptive structures are the one which undergo controlled shape changes to achieve a configuration which maximizes the efficiency of the structure in carrying the applied load. As a result of this improved efficiency, significant material and energy savings can be achieved.

My work was addressed towards refining the control problem of the structure and making it efficient for real-time control through an iterative linear control routine. I also established the accuracy of the converged solutions through Non-linear optimization algorithms such as SQP and IPM and analytical solutions obtained using Karush-Kunh-Tucker (KKT) condition. I also helped in assembly of prototype and callibration of its sensors.

Stress Ratio based Structural Optimization using Genetic Algorithm (July 2017 — August 2018)
Guide: Prof. Yogesh M. Desai, IIT Bombay
Prof. Venkata S K Delhi, IIT Bombay

Structural Optimization is defined as a discipline dealing with optimal design of load carrying structure. This project involved using Genetic Algorithm to optimize a Fiber Reinforced Plastic based structure with more than 13,000 members. Genetic Algorithm belongs to the larger class of evolutionary algorithm inspired by the process of natural selection.

A stress ratio based design function was employed for the Genetic Algorithm. STAAD-Pro model of the structure was used to analyze the structure and the result of the analysis were stored in excel sheets using the COM (Component Object Model) object of STAAD-Pro, OpenSTAAD. The data from the sheets was processed using VB.Net and the resulting modified cross sections were transmitted to the model via OpenSTAAD. The concepts of symmetry and anti-symmetry were also employed to simplify the structure and reduce the number of design variables in the optimization problem.

Technical Projects

Project Leaf: Team Shunya (August 2018 — presents)
Chief Advisor: Prof. Rangan Banerjee, IIT Bombay

The acronym LEAF stands for Light weight, Energy efficient Abode for the Future.The prototype model consists of an outer box, within which multiple flexible components are enclosed; this provides a large variety of spaces based on user requirements. The project strives to encourage community building and aid in self-built modular housing. Moreover, Project Leaf offers new opportunities for economic generation in small-scale households. The technical objectives of the project are directed towards developing a zero-impact house, which encompasses minimizing the energy use and negative impact that conventional buildings have on the environment. The use of renewable and natural energy resources ensures a reduction in construction and maintenance costs. Various technologies, methods, and materials have been identified to make the construction process faster and more efficient.

Project Solarise: Team Shunya (July 2017 — August 2018)
Chief Advisor: Prof. Rangan Banerjee, IIT Bombay

Project solarise was aimed at building a Net-Positive, fully-functional, 1,800 sq-ft (G + 1) house designed by integrating traditional architecture with contemporary interior designs, modern lifestyle and futuristic amenities. Passive solar architecture is the central design tenet with the aim of reducing the requirement for artificial thermal and lighting control as far as possible. Integrated with the concepts of modularity and ideas for quick assembly, the design ensures that fully-furnished house can be built within 20 days. The project is aligned with the Smart Cities Mission of the Government of India in setting examples and defining standards that can be replicated both within and outside the Smart City. This project was presented in Solar Decathlon China (SDC), 2018 edition, held in Dezhou, China.

Shape of Strongest column (July 2018 — November 2018)
Guide: Prof. Gopal R. Patil, IIT Bombay
In a prismatic column designed for compression with buckling or carrying bending moment along with compression, there are certain regions in the column which are underutilized. This project was directed towards finding the optimal shapes of the column under the applied load with compression and buckling constraints such that the efficiency of the column is maximized.

In this project, Artificial Neural Netwrok (ANN) based miniature face recognition application was developed. The face in the images were localized using Viola-Jones algorithm, followed by extraction and neural network-based face recognition with Gradient-based back-propagation algorithm.
